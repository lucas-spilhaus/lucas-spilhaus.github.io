My Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I'd like to develop skills as a developer and get a step into the industry.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

  • Strengths
    • Persistence: I'm able to keep working at an issue until I've developed/found a solution.
    • Motivated: I really enjoy the problem-solving of coding and thus commit myself to it much more than I otherwise would.
  • Weaknesses
    • Rarely ask for help: Generally speaking, I perfer to just keep trying on my own rather than reach out for help.
    • Procrastination: Self-explanatory, I am horrible with procrastinating.
    • Easily distracted: Ties in with procrastination, I'm very easily distracted and this is especially true when I'm not fully enjoying the current material.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

My social skills and confidence are something I’d like to improve on. I’ve always quite enjoyed engaging with and getting to know people, but I lack the confidence to act on this. Dev Academy makes a big deal out of getting to know your cohort during your course, so I see this as somewhat of an opportunity to improve those aspects of myself.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I will ensure that I commit myself to finishing this course and keeping up with my study plans. I believe mutual respect and understanding towards one another is incredibly important and will thus base myself on this during my time at Dev Academy. If I eventually am overwhelmed with the workload, I'll take a break and focus on something else to destress. If I reach a point where I cannot handle myself fully, I will reach out to others for support, be it facilitators or my own support network.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I’ll rely on researching my issue and if unable to deal with it through that, go to the Discord and ask for help via the appropriate channels.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

Regular check-ins/communication and the availability to reach out for help over specific topics. While I haven’t been doing this course for long, I’ve seen the Facilitation team carry out both with other members of my cohort and myself, albeit to a smaller extent.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I currently have no other major commitments outside of this course.
